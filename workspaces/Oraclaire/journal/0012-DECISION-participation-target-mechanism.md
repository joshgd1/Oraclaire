# DECISION D13: Participation Target and Mechanism

## Decision

Adopt Option 2 (justify 40% with mechanisms) as the architecture target. Adopt Option 1 (20% data-supported floor) as the Sprint 1 ship target. Four mechanisms adopted to close the gap between Sprint 1 and architecture target.

## Sprint 1 Target: 20% Sustained Participation

- 20% sustained is achievable without mechanisms that push toward 40%
- 20% is the floor where the model produces useful (if noisy) scores for a pilot deployment
- Below 20%, the model cannot reliably calibrate risk tiers
- Source: ethical analysis (opt-in gives 30–50% initial, declining without intervention) + user personas (5–10% by month six without value delivery)

## Architecture Target: 40% Sustained Over 12 Cycles

- Below 40% participation, the sample is no longer representative of the workforce
- Below 30%, the model cannot reliably distinguish burnout signal from self-selection bias
- The model needs longitudinal data (same employees across multiple cycles) to detect trajectory
- Source: original frame §5 rationale (representativeness floor)

## Four Mechanisms Adopted

### A. Weekly Pulse (10 seconds, 1 question)

- Single question drawn from CBI item pool, rotating weekly
- Employee sees their own trend over time — personal value, not organisational surveillance
- No HR visibility on individual pulse responses — aggregated team trend only
- Purpose: maintain touch between monthly CBI cycles, reduce survey fatigue, provide longitudinal signal

### B. Monthly CBI (19 items, ~3 minutes)

- Full Copenhagen Burnout Inventory — the validated instrument that is Sprint 1's primary signal
- This is the model's training data — the pulse supplements but does not replace it
- Employee sees their own scores with SHAP decomposition after each cycle

### C. Employee-First 24-Hour Data Visibility Gate

- After each assessment cycle, employees see their own results 24 hours before HR
- Hardcoded delay — not configurable by the organisation
- Purpose: the employee owns their data first. Employees who feel the tool works for them, not for HR, participate at higher rates

### D. SHAP-Matched Curated Content Library

- After each assessment, employees receive 2–3 curated resources matched to their top SHAP factors
- Content mapped to risk tier: Low (self-guided), Moderate (team resources), High/Critical (professional pathways)
- Purpose: every assessment delivers immediate personal value, not just a score

## What Was Rejected

- 40% sustained as Sprint 1 target with no evidence it is achievable (aspiration without mechanism)
- Lowering the architecture target below 40% (sacrifices model representativeness)
- Making the 24-hour employee-first gate configurable by the organisation (defeats the participation mechanism)
- SHAP-matched content generated by LLM (curation quality risk — use vetted library)
- HR visibility on individual weekly pulse responses (would destroy trust)

## Trade-Offs Accepted

- Sprint 1 ships at 20% — the model is noisier than the 40% architecture allows. Pilot data will be less representative but sufficient for initial calibration
- The four mechanisms add scope to the build. Each mechanism is a separate todo in the Sprint 1 backlog
- The 24-hour employee-first gate is a rigid product constraint — it cannot be waived for any customer, even if requested. This limits configurability

## Consequences

- Sprint 1 pilot participation will be tracked against both targets: 20% (must clear) and 40% (directional progress)
- If Sprint 1 pilot shows <20% sustained, the participation mechanisms need redesign before scaling
- If Sprint 1 pilot shows >30% sustained, the architecture target may be achievable by Sprint 2

## Frame §5 Changes

- Replace "40% sustained participation over 12 assessment cycles" with two-level structure
- Add "Sprint 1 ship target: 20% sustained participation"
- Add "Architecture target: 40% sustained participation over 12 assessment cycles"
- Add §5.1 Participation Mechanisms with all four mechanisms (A through D)
- Update founder-owned calls: participation rate floor confirmed per DECISION 13

## For Discussion

1. If Sprint 1 pilot data shows sustained participation below 15%, does the product pivot to team-aggregate-only (abandoning individual scoring) — or does it double down on the participation mechanisms?
2. The weekly pulse rotates through CBI items. If the pulse captures the same employees every week but the monthly CBI captures a different (non-overlapping) subset, are we training the model on two different populations? Does the pulse data need to be held out from model training?
3. Counterfactual: if the 24-hour employee-first gate were removed (HR sees scores immediately), would participation drop meaningfully — or is the gate a founder intuition that the data will not validate?
