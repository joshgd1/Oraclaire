# Oraclaire — Customer Acceptable Use Policy

**Document type:** Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)
**Applies to:** All customers and authorised users of the Oraclaire platform
**Effective date:** 2026-05-16

---

## 1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") defines the permissible uses of the Oraclaire workplace wellbeing analytics platform. By accessing or using Oraclaire, the customer ("you") and your authorised users agree to comply with this policy.

---

## 2. Permitted Uses

Oraclaire is designed exclusively for **organisational wellbeing purposes**. Permitted uses include:

- Assessing employee burnout risk using validated survey instruments (Copenhagen Burnout Inventory)
- Aggregating team-level wellbeing indicators for HR planning and intervention
- Identifying individual employees in the Critical risk tier for human HR review
- Providing employees with their own burnout risk scores and plain-language explanations
- Compliance with applicable workplace health and safety obligations

---

## 3. Prohibited Uses

The following uses of Oraclaire are **strictly prohibited**:

### 3.1 Employment Decisions

| Prohibited Use                                                 | Rationale                                                                                                    |
| -------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Using Oraclaire scores for hiring or termination decisions     | Oraclaire is not validated for selection or dismissal purposes; doing so constitutes unlawful discrimination |
| Using scores for performance ratings or performance management | Risk tiers reflect wellbeing, not competence or output                                                       |
| Using scores for promotions, transfers, or pay decisions       | Wellbeing data must not influence employment status                                                          |
| Using scores to deny accommodations                            | Wellbeing classifications are not a basis for employment accommodations decisions                            |

### 3.2 Unauthorized Disclosure

| Prohibited Use                                                               | Rationale                                                                       |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Sharing an individual employee's score with their manager or peers           | Individual scores are confidential between Oraclaire and the employee           |
| Publishing individual scores publicly or on internal communication platforms | Violates employee privacy rights                                                |
| Using score data in legal proceedings without explicit consent               | Unauthorized disclosure of health-related data                                  |
| Attempting to re-identify employees from aggregated data                     | Even de-identified data covering small populations may enable re-identification |

### 3.3 Technical Prohibitions

| Prohibited Use                                               | Rationale                                                |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| Reverse-engineering the scoring model                        | Model intellectual property; analysis constitutes breach |
| Attempting to extract raw survey responses from the platform | Raw responses are not accessible via API or UI           |
| Bypassing or disabling access controls                       | Violation of access control architecture                 |
| Using automated scripts or bots to access the platform       | Platform is intended for human interaction only          |
| Attempting to modify or tamper with risk scores              | Integrity of classification records is essential         |

---

## 4. Customer Responsibilities

### 4.1 Employee Notice

You are responsible for providing employees with clear notice before they participate in any assessment, including:

- The purpose of the assessment
- How data will be used and who will have access
- Employee rights including access, explanation, and withdrawal
- The 24-hour employee-first visibility delay

### 4.2 Works Council Consultation

In applicable jurisdictions (Germany, France, and other EU member states), you are responsible for completing any required works council or employee representative consultation **before** deploying Oraclaire. Proof of consultation may be required before deployment.

### 4.3 Accurate Data

You are responsible for ensuring HRIS data (tenure, seniority, company type, WFH setup) provided to Oraclaire is accurate and up to date. Inaccurate HRIS data produces incorrect risk classifications.

### 4.4 Participation Rate

You are responsible for encouraging participation to achieve the participation target (40% minimum). Low participation rates reduce the accuracy of both individual and aggregate risk estimates.

---

## 5. Oraclaire Responsibilities

Oraclaire will:

- Maintain technical controls that block score export to HRIS or performance systems
- Provide employees with plain-language explanations of their scores
- Maintain audit logs of all data access and modification events
- Notify you of any data breach involving your employee data within 72 hours
- Support your compliance with applicable data protection regulations

---

## 6. Enforcement

### 6.1 Violations

Violations of this AUP may result in:

- Suspension of access to the Oraclaire platform
- Termination of your service agreement
- Referral to relevant regulatory authorities

### 6.2 Reporting

If you become aware of a violation of this policy, you must notify Oraclaire immediately at `security@oraclaire.io`.

---

## 7. Suspension and Termination

Oraclaire reserves the right to suspend or terminate access without notice if:

- Use of the platform poses an imminent risk to employee safety
- There is evidence of systematic prohibited use
- A data breach occurs due to customer-side actions
- The customer fails to remediate a violation within 30 days of notice

---

Questions about this Acceptable Use Policy should be directed to `legal@oraclaire.io`.
